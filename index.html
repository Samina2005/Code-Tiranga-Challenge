Heroes of Indian Freedom

Mahatma Gandhi

(1869 - 1948)

The Father of the Nation, Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi led India's independence movement through non-violent civil disobedience. His principles of Satyagraha and Ahimsa inspired freedom movements worldwide. He organized major campaigns like the Salt March and Quit India Movement, proving that freedom could be won without violence.

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose

(1897 - 1945)

A charismatic leader who believed in armed resistance against British rule. He formed the Indian National Army (INA) and gave the famous slogan "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!" His approach differed from Gandhi's non-violence, but his dedication to India's freedom was unwavering.

Bhagat Singh

(1907 - 1931)

A revolutionary socialist who became a folk hero for his fearless fight against British rule. At just 23, he was executed for his revolutionary activities. His famous words "Inquilab Zindabad" (Long Live Revolution) continue to inspire generations. He believed that sacrifice was necessary for freedom.

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

(1875 - 1950)

Known as the "Iron Man of India," he played a crucial role in India's independence movement and later became the first Deputy Prime Minister. He successfully integrated over 500 princely states into the Indian Union, earning him the title "Architect of Modern India."

Rani Lakshmibai

(1828 - 1858)

The brave Queen of Jhansi who fought valiantly against British rule during the Indian Rebellion of 1857. She led her troops from the front and died fighting for her motherland. Her courage and sacrifice made her an icon of resistance and women's empowerment in Indian history.

Jawaharlal Nehru

(1889 - 1964)

India's first Prime Minister and a key figure in the independence movement. Known as "Pandit Nehru," he worked closely with Gandhi and played a vital role in shaping modern India. His vision of a secular, democratic, and socialist India became the foundation of the nation's development.
